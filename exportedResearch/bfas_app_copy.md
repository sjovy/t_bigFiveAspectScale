## üß¨ BACKGROUND INFO

### What is BFAS?

The **Big Five Aspect Scale** measures personality across **5 major dimensions** and **10 specific aspects**, providing more nuanced insights than traditional personality tests.

Developed by personality researchers Colin DeYoung, Lena Quilty, and Jordan Peterson (2007), BFAS reveals how two distinct aspects combine within each personality dimension.

### The 10 Aspects

**Openness/Intellect**
- Openness: Aesthetic appreciation, imagination
- Intellect: Abstract thinking, intellectual curiosity

**Conscientiousness**
- Industriousness: Achievement drive, self-discipline
- Orderliness: Organization, structure preference

**Extraversion**
- Enthusiasm: Social warmth, positive emotion
- Assertiveness: Leadership, dominance

**Agreeableness**
- Compassion: Empathy, caring for others
- Politeness: Respect, conflict avoidance

**Neuroticism**
- Withdrawal: Anxiety, behavioral inhibition
- Volatility: Emotional reactivity, irritability

### Why Aspects Matter

Two people with the same "Conscientiousness" score might be very different:
- **Person A:** Highly driven but disorganized
- **Person B:** Perfectly organized but low motivation

BFAS captures these crucial distinctions.
